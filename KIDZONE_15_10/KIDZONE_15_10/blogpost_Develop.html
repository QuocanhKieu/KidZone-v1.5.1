Develop financial literacy with Kids-Zone's money games

Posted on 3/31/2023

It’s never too early to start teaching your learner all about smart money management—starting with the monetary values and names of U.S. currency! We’ve collected our favorite money-minded games on Kids-Zone to help you introduce or review the concept of currency to your child.

It just makes cents! Learn about Kids-Zone’s money games:

Break the Bank - Counting and Sorting

Despite the name, this game doesn’t cost a thing! But you will have to break open some piggy banks to play. Break the Bank teaches learners the names, values, and presidential portraits associated with U.S. coins and bills. To play, your learner will start by smashing open a piggy bank and counting or sorting the money inside!

In the Break the Bank - Counting, students will have to correctly count the amount of money on their screen four times to unlock a cash grab game. In Break the Bank - Sorting, learners will have to sort each coin or bill into their correct values at the bottom of the screen. Once they successfully count or sort all the money, they’ll have a chance to collect as many falling coins as possible in their piggy bank.

Learn to Count Money

Practice making exact change in this undersea treasure hunt! To play, your learner will click and drag coins and bills into their treasure chest.

Learners can adjust the game difficulty in order to customize the game to their learning level. In beginner mode, students can use as many coins or bills as they’d like to reach the specified amount of money. In expert mode, they’ll have to use the least number of coins and bills to reach the total.

The underwater theme makes this game extra fun. For every amount your learner gets correct, they’ll earn one fish. Once they earn 10 fish, they can move on to the next level. But beware, for every question they answer incorrectly, they’ll lose a fish. Students will love building up their own personal aquarium filled with treasure!

Money BINGO

Play the classic game of BINGO with a monetary twist. In Money BINGO, your learner will be shown different combinations of coins and bills, and practice counting them up and selecting the correct amount of money. Students are able to customize which coins and bills are used as well as the board size to allow for more personalized practice. For every BINGO your learner earns, they’ll receive a new BINGO bug! Great for students in first through fifth grade, who will be excited to complete their BINGO bug collection.

Dolphin Dash - Counting Money

Race to the finish line in this mad dash to complete multiple choice problems. Dolphin Dash - Counting Money is a multiplayer race that measures the accuracy and speed at which students can identify the value of groups of coins. Learners can play alone against the computer, or start an online race with friends or classmates to see who can answer the most money questions the fastest.

To play, students will be shown a couple of coins and can choose the correct value from multiple choice answers. At the end of the race, students can review how accurately and quickly they answered, and which coins they should review more. Second through fourth graders will love racing against their friends to see who comes out on top!
