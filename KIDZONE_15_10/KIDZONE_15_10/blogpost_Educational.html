Educational games for older learners

Posted on 6/2/2023

Looking for entertaining educational activities for your older learner? Look no further than Kids-Zone! We’ve rounded up the best games to get your students’ brains working while they play.

These activities are a great way to develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills for 4th graders and up! Even adults can get in on the fun.

Read on to find out with games are the perfect puzzles for advanced learners:

Brain Trainer

Strengthen your mind with Brain Trainer! These fast-paced brain teasers help your learner build up skills to improve brain function while they play. Through eight minigames, learners will improve their memory, reaction time, logical reasoning, and focus. Have kids start their day with the Daily Challenge for a quick warm-up before learning, or let them play through training mode to focus on a specific skill. Plus, fun progress tracking helps kids visualize how much they’ve improved!

Colorush

Practice logical reasoning in this playful puzzle. The goal of Colorush is simple—get each colorful particle from the container to the matching capacitor. To do this, your learner will adjust the triangular blocks and create a clear path for the particle. As they progress, more colors and block types will be added to increase the difficulty. These new items prompt learners to develop their attention to detail and understanding of sequencing to help them solve the puzzle. With 32 levels to play through, your learner will have fun puzzling over these problems for hours.

Blue Box

Put your problem-solving skills to the test in Blue Box. To win, your learner must move the bouncing blue box from the green square to the red square, destroying all the other boxes as they go. Students will develop pattern recognition and logical thinking skills as they work their way through 60 unique puzzles. This engaging game will spark your learner’s curiosity while advancing their critical thinking abilities.

Math Man

Train your math fact fluency in this classic arcade game. This Pac-Man style game will help learners practice quickly solving addition, subtraction, multiplication and division problems. To play, learners will direct Math Man to eat question marks that reveal the problem. Then, they’ll have to catch the ghost with the correct answer before time runs out. This game encourages learners to practice their fact fluency and develop their reaction time in a fun and engaging environment.

Zero Sum

Apply your math knowledge and logic in this drag-and-drop activity! Using spatial reasoning and fact fluency, Zero Sum challenges learners to zero out all the given number sets using blocks of smaller values. If learners get a block below zero they’ll have to start the puzzle over. With 20 puzzles to play through, learners have plenty of opportunities to practice their math recall and logic skills.
