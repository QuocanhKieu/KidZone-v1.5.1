Games to play with the whole class

Posted on 5/3/2023

Want to increase class participation? Try playing a game!

We’ve picked out some of the best Kids-Zone games that the whole class can play, either on an interactive whiteboard or by projecting from a computer screen. Whether you’re reviewing an old topic or introducing a new one, students will love to take charge and show off their knowledge!

Read on for the best games to play as a group:

Learn to Tell Time

Teach your students to tell time with giant interactive clocks! Great for early learners, your class will review hours, minutes, AM, and PM on either an analog or a digital clock. Students will love coming up to the board to drag the minute hand around, or click up and down to set the clock to the right time.

Learn to Tell Time increases in difficulty through four stages, starting with telling time by the hour. And, with 10 different prompts per stage, all your students will have the opportunity to participate!

Seasonal Shuffle

Put the calendar months in order with Seasonal Shuffle. In this game, pre-kindergarten through second grade students will learn the order of the months, which season they belong to, and the biggest holidays associated with each month. To make it an engaging class activity, have your students come up one by one to put a month or holiday in the correct order.

Out of Sight Words

Practice recognizing sight words with a giant game of I Spy! Through 10 colorful illustrated puzzles, learners will practice matching words from the Dolch Word List to their corresponding images. The Dolch Sight Words are a list of 220 English words that children can learn to recognize on sight. This early reading game is great for learners in kindergarten through third grade. Your class will have so much fun picking out which puzzles to solve and finding each word!

Submarine Spelling Practice

Let students show off their spelling abilities in Submarine Spelling Practice! In this game, learners will practice spelling pre-kindergarten through third grade level Dolch Sight Words. Include your whole class by having students take turns dragging and dropping letters into the correct order. Make sure your sound is on so learners can hear the word they’re trying to spell!

Get in Shape - Geometry

Help your class get used to recognizing geometric shapes within real-life scenes! This fun twist on an I Spy game reinforces the basics of geometry by connecting concepts to real-life examples. Plus, the three-dimensional aspect allows you to explain complex shapes, like triangular prisms, by breaking them into easier to understand two-dimensional parts. With over 80 shapes to find, all of your students will get a chance to play!

Orbital Order

Learn about the solar system in this out-of-this-world trivia game! Orbital Order is a great opportunity for students to work together in teams to demonstrate their understanding of each planet in the solar system and the sun.

If they answer three questions about a planet correctly, they’ll be able to place it in its correct order in the solar system. Perfect for students in fourth through sixth grade, this game would make a great end-of-unit review, or a fun introduction to facts about outer space!
