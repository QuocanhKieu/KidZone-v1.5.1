Top 7 typing games to try

Posted on 7/6/2023

Typing has become an essential skill for students to master along their learning journey. Whether you’re just starting to introduce your young learner to typing, or have a more advanced student who wants to increase their words per minute, Kids-Zone has the perfect typing game to make learning fun!

These games help learners master typing basics, memorize the QWERTY keyboard, and increase their typing speed.

Read on to find the right game for your typist:

Games to introduce typing:

Keyboarding Zoo and Keyboarding Zoo 2

Get the hang of typing basics, and hang out with some monkeys, in Keyboarding Zoo! Perfect for students who are new to typing, this game teaches learners to type individual letters. Make sure that the sound is on!

If your student is ready for the next level, they can move onto Keyboarding Zoo 2! This sequel teaches students to use the shift key, space bar, and period key. Learners will have a chance to practice typing both short words and short sentences.

Typing Rocket Jr.

Test your typing speed in Typing Rocket Jr.! In this game, learners will build up their muscle memory, reaction time, and hand-eye coordination. Great for students in kindergarten through 2nd grade, they will have three minutes to type as many letters flying by as possible before time runs out.

If your learner is ready for more of a challenge, have them try Typing Rocket! Students will only have one minute to type, making the game extra exciting.

Games for intermediate typists:

Cup Stacking

Learn how to type backwards and forwards in Cup Stacking. In this game, K-5 students will practice using the home row (keys: “ASDF” and “JKL”) as they type a group of letters. As they play, learners will build-up their muscle memory from typing a list of letters, once in order and then again in reverse.

Keyboard Challenge

Memorize the standard QWERTY Keyboard with Keyboard Challenge! In this technological spin on a puzzle, learners are challenged to put the keys of a keyboard back in the right spots. Students can choose between completing the full keyboard or only the numbers and letters. Great for students in 2nd grade and up, this game helps learners to establish a better understanding of their keyboard layout.

Games for advanced typists:

Owl Planes Typing

Put your typing and spelling to the test! Owl Planes typing is a fast-paced multiplayer race to type the fastest. Perfect for students in 3rd grade and up, this game improves reaction time, muscle memory, and typing accuracy. To win, learners will have to accurately type as fast as possible. At the end of each round, students can review their accuracy rate, words they mistyped, and their estimated words per minute.

Koala Paddleboards - Spelling Practice

Koala Paddleboards is for learners who are ready to take on a typing challenge. To play, students will have to accurately type the word they hear as fast as possible. Like Owl Planes, this game develops learners’ reaction time and muscle memory. The added challenge is for students to type accurately without seeing the word in front of them. The best part? Learners can play together! Make sure your sound is on.
